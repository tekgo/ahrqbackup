Diagnostic flexible bronchoscopy: proportion of adults undergoing bronchoscopy who suffer a serious adverse event.

Brief Abstract

Description

This measure is used to assess the proportion of adults undergoing bronchoscopy who suffer a serious adverse event.

Rationale

This standard will enable a prospective evaluation of complications at diagnostic bronchoscopy (refer to Appendix 1 of the original measure documentation), and taken together with additional standards relating to efficacy, safe sedation and patient experience will provide much needed information on the quality of bronchoscopy services in the United Kingdom (UK). In time, these data may prove useful in setting appropriate targets for audit/quality.

The British Thoracic Society (BTS) guideline for diagnostic flexible bronchoscopy in adults (Du Rand et al., 2013) and the BTS guideline for advanced diagnostic and therapeutic flexible bronchoscopy in adults (Du Rand et al., 2011) are the main references for the quality standards.

Numerator Inclusions/Exclusions

Inclusions
Number of adults undergoing bronchoscopy who suffer a serious adverse event

Note: Suggested serious adverse events include:

  • Severe bleeding (refer to Table 1 in the original measure documentation for a classification of bleeding during bronchoscopy)
  • Cardiac arrhythmia requiring treatment
  • Seizures
  • Myocardial infarction/pulmonary oedema
  • Pneumothorax requiring aspiration/intercostal drain
  • Oversedation requiring ventilatory support or reversal
  • Hospitalisation
  • Admission to intensive care unit
  • Death

Refer to Appendix 1 of the original measure documentation for additional information.
