Brief Abstract

Description

This measure is used to assess the overall satisfaction of family members of patients in the intensive care unit based upon their responses to the Family Satisfaction in the Intensive Care Unit© (FS-ICU 24) questionnaire.

Rationale

In the intensive care unit (ICU), patient-centered care includes family-centered care. Since most ICU patients cannot make decisions for themselves, families are often involved as surrogate decision makers. Therefore, the perspectives of family and other surrogate decision makers are especially important in the critical care setting, and family satisfaction is an important outcome measure.

Families have an important role in sharing relevant information, for example: what transpired before the admission; what kind of person the patient is; and, perhaps most importantly, what the patient would want in terms of care, given the current situation.

A key domain of quality of care is satisfaction with care. Because desirable health status outcomes may not be attained after critical illness and because mortality rates in tertiary referral ICUs may approach 15% to 20%, satisfaction with care is important not only to patients but also to their families. Furthermore, given that the majority of critically ill patients (especially those at high risk for poor outcomes) are unable to participate in decision making about treatments, the family's perspective is central to understanding (and measuring) satisfaction with care in the ICU.

Denominator Inclusions/Exclusions

Inclusions
Family members of intensive care unit patients who made at least one visit and completed* the Family Satisfaction in the Intensive Care Unit© (FS-ICU 24) questionnaire

*"Completed" surveys are defined as those with at least 70% of the items in the respective scale completed.

Exclusions
Unspecified

Relationship of Denominator to Numerator

Denominator (Index) Event

Denominator Time Window

Numerator Inclusions/Exclusions

Inclusions
Respondents' answers to the Family Satisfaction in the Intensive Care Unit© (FS-ICU 24) questionnaire. From the responses, a scale score is calculated. On this scale, 0 is "least satisfied" and 100 is "most satisfied."

Refer to the Family Satisfaction in the Intensive Care Unit© (FS-ICU): Instructions for Recoding and Scoring document listed in the "Companion Documents" field for additional information on scoring.

Exclusions
Questionnaires with less than 70% of the items in the "Satisfaction with Care" scale and the "Family Satisfaction with Decision-making Around Care of Critically Ill Patients" are excluded from scoring analysis.

Evaluation of Measure Properties

Extent of Measure Testing

The Family Satisfaction in the Intensive Care Unit© (FS-ICU 24) questionnaire is a valid and reliable tool for assessing family satisfaction in the intensive care unit (ICU). Scores on the FS-ICU show good validity against other indicators of ICU quality.
