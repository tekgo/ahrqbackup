Brief Abstract

Description

This measure is used to assess the percentage of all patient-months for patients whose delivered dose of dialysis (either hemodialysis or peritoneal dialysis) met the specified threshold during the reporting period.

This Kt/V dialysis adequacy comprehensive clinical measure includes the following four components:

  • Delivered dose of hemodialysis above minimum (see the related National Quality Measures Clearinghouse [NQMC] summary)
  • Delivered dose of peritoneal dialysis above minimum (see the related NQMC summary)
  • Minimum single pool (sp)Kt/V for pediatric hemodialysis patients (see the related NQMC summary)
  • Delivered dose of pediatric peritoneal dialysis above minimum (see the related NQMC summary)

Rationale

The primary rationale for this combined measure is to make more facilities eligible for public reporting of these metrics by meeting the greater than 11 eligible patients restriction. For public reporting on Dialysis Facility Compare (DFC) and the End Stage Renal Disease (ESRD) Quality Incentive Program (QIP), a facility has to treat at least 11 qualifying patients for each measure in order to receive a score on that measure. The 11 patient requirement is anchored in Health and Human Services (HHS) policy, related to small cell sizes to protect identification of patients and release of protected health information. An additional reason is the need for sufficient data to achieve reliability of a measure calculation for less than 11 patients. The developer recognizes there is no published evidence describing use of the combined subpopulation and modality measures. However, each component measure has strong evidence support from literature and each also reflects consensus guideline recommendations. Combining these established consensus measures to counter an unintended consequence of the application of federal protected health information regulations should not require additional scientific justification beyond what already exists.

In the case of dialysis adequacy, the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) found that a significant number of facilities that have less than 11 peritoneal dialysis (PD) patients or less than 11 pediatric patients would be included in the new combined measures but excluded from the individual measure, leading to the systematic exclusion of these facilities from assessment on these measures because of the reporting requirements.

To account for this, CMS proposed the three new measures that assess dialysis adequacy by modality that includes both adult and pediatric populations, and an overall measure of all adult and pediatric hemodialysis and peritoneal dialysis patients. CMS also seeks maintenance endorsement of the individual measures previously endorsed, based on the same level of evidence presented for those measures. It is CMS's intention to eventually retire the individual measures once the combined measures are endorsed and implemented.

Denominator Description

  • All adult hemodialysis patients who received dialysis greater than two and less than four times a week (adults, greater than or equal to 18 years old), and all pediatric in-center hemodialysis patients who received dialysis greater than two and less than four times a week (pediatric, less than 18 years), and did not indicate frequent dialysis

Denominator Inclusions/Exclusions

Inclusions

  • All adult hemodialysis patients who received dialysis greater than two and less than four times a week (adults, greater than or equal to 18 years old), and all pediatric in-center hemodialysis patients who received dialysis greater than two and less than four times a week (pediatric, less than 18 years), and did not indicate frequent dialysis
  • All patients (both hemodialysis and peritoneal dialysis) who are assigned to the facility for the entire month and have had end stage renal disease (ESRD) for 90 days or more

Note:

  • A patient may only be assigned to one dialysis facility each month.
  • If selected Kt/V value is missing or 9.99 (i.e., when using claims), include patient-month in the denominator, but not in the numerator.
  • If selected Kt/V value meets the Kt/V value threshold (greater than or equal to 1.2 for hemodialysis, greater than or equal to 1.7 for adult PD, or less than or equal to 1.8 for pediatric peritoneal dialysis), then include patient-month in denominator and numerator.

Exclusions

  • Hemodialysis patients receiving dialysis less than or equal to 2 times weekly or greater than or equal to 4 times weekly
  • Pediatric home hemodialysis patients
  • Patients on ESRD treatment for fewer than 90 days at the beginning of the reporting month when using CROWNWeb. If claims are used, the 90 days is determined based on the Claim-from date.
  • Patients who changed dialysis modality during the month. Note: For adult hemodialysis patients, a change from in-center to home hemodialysis (or vice versa) is not considered a modality change.
  • Patients who were not assigned to the facility for the entire month due to death or discharge for one of the following reasons: discontinued, involuntary discharge, transplant, or other reasons for leaving dialysis
  • Patients who were not assigned to the facility for the entire month due to transfer to a different facility

Exclusions/Exceptions

not defined yet

Numerator Inclusions/Exclusions

Inclusions
Number of patient-months in the denominator for patients whose delivered dose of dialysis met the specified thresholds. The thresholds are as follows:

  • Hemodialysis (all ages): single pool (sp)Kt/V greater than or equal to 1.2 (calculated from the last measurement of the month using Urea Kinetic Modeling [UKM] or Daugirdas II)
  • Peritoneal dialysis (pediatric less than 18 years old): Kt/V greater than or equal to 1.8 (dialytic + residual, measured within the past six months)
  • Peritoneal dialysis (adult greater than or equal to 18 years old): Kt/V greater than or equal to 1.7 (dialytic + residual, measured within the past four months)
