Sarcoma: proportion of patients with extremity sarcoma who undergo a primary limb-sparing surgery.

Brief Abstract

Description

This measure is used to assess the proportion of patients with extremity sarcoma who undergo a primary limb-sparing surgery.

Note from the National Quality Measures Clearinghouse: This measure is part of the Cancer Quality Performance Indicators (QPIs) collection. For more information, including a complete list of QPI measure sets, please visit the Healthcare Improvement Scotland Web site.

Rationale

Studies have shown that surgical treatment for approximately 90% to 95% of patients involves limb sparing surgery (Misra et al., 2009; Megerle & Sauerbier, 2011; Ghert et al., 2005).

Rates of amputation have decreased over the years and is typically reserved for patients with locally advanced disease that cannot be managed by limb sparing surgery (Merimsky et al., 2005; Williard et al., 1992).

Patients who undergo limb sparing surgery have reportedly improved quality of life post treatment, uncompromised survival rates and local tumour control. As well as, an asymptomatic and functional limb (Ghert et al., 2005; Williard et al., 1992; Muhic et al., 2008).
