Brief Abstract

Description

This measure is used to assess the percent of eligible patients with severe sepsis or septic shock who were started on activated protein C (APC) within 48 hours following severe sepsis/septic shock identification.

Rationale

Studies show that annually there are between 500,000 to one million cases of sepsis and severe sepsis in American hospitals. The annual mortality rate for these cases is between 15 and 30 percent or as many as 200,000 deaths. Many more patients suffer from permanent organ damage. The cost to society in dollars spent and lives lost prematurely is enormous. While there are many useful clinical interventions, research shows that they are applied inconsistently, if at all.

In a large, well-conducted, randomized trial, the use of activated protein C in patients with severe sepsis was associated with a five percent absolute reduction in hospital mortality and a six percent reduction in 28-day mortality. The risk of bleeding was similar to heparin. While the mortality benefits of this drug are clear, hospitals disagree over exactly which patients should receive this therapy and in which patients the risk/benefit ratio is favorable. For now, most facilities are relying on their own protocols. There are a number of large cohort studies under way to help clarify which patients should receive this therapy.

Costs

A study confirmed that patients with severe sepsis consume significant resources. The average hospital length of stay was 20 days at an average cost of $22,100. National cost estimates for the care of severe sepsis based on this study is $16.7 billion dollars, with the care of patients older than 65 costing $8.7 billion (52.3 percent), and care of those older than 75 costs $5.1 billion dollars (30.8 percent). The costs for caring for patients with sepsis are projected to rise approximately 1.5 percent per year due to the aging U.S. population.

Denominator Inclusions/Exclusions

Inclusions
Total number of eligible* patients with a diagnosis of severe sepsis or septic shock

*Eligibility is determined by inclusion/exclusion guidelines at your institution.

Note: Refer to original measure documentation for definitions and additional details.

Exclusions
Any one of the following:

  • Patients less than 16 years of age
  • Patients not eligible to receive activated protein C (APC)
  • Not Applicable because care was withdrawn or patient expired within 48 hours following severe sepsis/septic shock identification
  • Date or Time of severe sepsis/septic shock identification unknown
  • Cases with a time elapsed EARLIER THAN -24 hours or GREATER THAN +96 hours. Time elapsed is the difference between severe sepsis/septic shock identification and the administration of APC.

Relationship of Denominator to Numerator

All cases in the denominator are equally eligible to appear in the numerator
