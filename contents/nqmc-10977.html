Title

Pulmonary resection: percentage of patients undergoing pulmonary resection whose smoking history was documented preoperatively.

Brief Abstract

Description

This measure is used to assess the percentage of patients undergoing pulmonary resection whose smoking history was documented preoperatively.

Rationale

More than 30,000 patients undergo pulmonary resection (not including diagnostic lung biopsies) each year in the United States (Memtsoudis et al., 2006). Despite this large number of surgical procedures, there remain to date no standard criteria to measure the quality of care received by patients undergoing pulmonary resection. The general thoracic surgical team developed an a priori set of patient-centered quality of care measures, specific to patients undergoing pulmonary resection.

Smoking history is a vital part of a complete history in patients with pulmonary disease and especially in those being considered for possible lung resection. As part of the diagnostic search for indeterminate pulmonary processes, the patient's smoking history plays an integral role as smoking is implicated in more than 90% of lung cancer cases (Alberg & Samet, 2003). The preoperative knowledge of the patient's smoking history is, therefore, essential to properly plan any potential intervention including pulmonary resection. Smoking history is useful in identifying patients who may be at increased risk for postoperative complications due to diminished pulmonary reserve in general and impaired mucociliary clearance in particular (Warner, 2006).
