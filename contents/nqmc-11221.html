Brief Abstract

Description

This measure is used to assess the proportion of patients with invasive breast cancer who have a greater than or equal to 5% overall survival benefit of chemotherapy treatment predicted at 10 years that undergo adjuvant chemotherapy.

Note from the National Quality Measures Clearinghouse: This measure is part of the Cancer Quality Performance Indicators (QPIs) collection. For more information, including a complete list of QPI measure sets, please visit the Healthcare Improvement Scotland Web site.

Rationale

Large randomised trials have confirmed that adjuvant systemic therapy improves relapse-free survival and overall survival (NHS Quality Improvement Scotland, 2008).

Clinical trials have demonstrated that adjuvant drug treatments substantially reduce 5-year recurrence rates and 15-year mortality rates (Early Breast Cancer Trialists' Collaborative Group [EBCTCG], 2005).

Success of treatment is based on a number of different factors including tumour size, grade and involvement of lymph nodes. Prognostic tools such as PREDICT assist clinicians and patients to make informed decisions on appropriate treatment by predicting survival and determining those patients likely to benefit from adjuvant treatment (Wishart et al., 2010; Wishart et al., 2012).

Denominator Inclusions/Exclusions

Inclusions
All patients with invasive breast cancer who have a greater than or equal to 5% overall survival benefit of chemotherapy treatment predicted at 10 years

Note: The validated tool PREDICT should be used to calculate predicted benefit of adjuvant chemotherapy.

Exclusions

  • All patients with breast cancer taking part in trials of chemotherapy treatment
  • All patients with breast cancer who have had neo-adjuvant chemotherapy
  • All patients with M1 disease
